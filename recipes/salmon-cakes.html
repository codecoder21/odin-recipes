Salmon Cakes

Description

This recipe is a family favorite! It's hearty, flavorful, and a great alternative to hamburgers. This recipe is also very simple and easy to prep and cook! There are a lot of people from what the reviews are saying, that you can also do a good amount of nice varitions to the Salmon Cakes. Substitute cubed cooked potatoes for the bread, if you like.

Ingredients

  • 1 (14.75 ounce) can salmon, undrained and flaked
  • 1 slice of bread, shredded
  • 1 small onion, grated
  • 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  • 1 egg
  • salt and pepper for taste
  • 1 cup of vegetable oil for frying

Steps

  1. Mix together salmon, bread, onion, all-purpose flour, egg, salt and pepper. Shape into four patties.
  2. In a large skillet heat oil over high heat. Cook the patties until browned on both sides, about 8 minutes. Drain briefly on paper towels before serving.

The Real Mojito

Description

This is an authentic recipe for Mojito. It is sized perfectly for one serving, but of course you can adjust it accordingly and make a pitcher full! It's a very refreshing drink for hot summer days or if you're just relaxing on an off day. Tonic water can be substituted instead of the soda water but the taste is different and somewhat bitter.

Ingredients

  • 10 fresh mint leaves
  • ½ lime, cut into 4 wedges
  • 2 tbs white sugar, or to taste
  • 1 cup of ice cubes
  • 1 ½ fluid ounces white rum
  • ½ club soda

Steps

  1. Place mint leaves and 1 lime wedge into a sturdy glass. Use a muddler to crush the mint and lime to release the mint oils and lime juice. Add 2 more lime wedges and the sugar, and muddle again to release the lime juice. Do not strain the mixture. Fill the glass almost to the top with ice. Pour the rum over the ice, and fill the glass with carbonated water. Stir, taste, and add more sugar if desired. Garnish with the remaining lime wedge.

The Best Chocolate Chip Cookies

Description

This is the perfect chocolate chip cookie! They have the right balance of flavor and texture. Crispy on the outside and chewy on the inside. For added flavor, try adding a cup of white chocolate chips!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tsp hot water
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Cream together the butter, white sugar, and brown sugar until smooth. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla. Dissolve baking soda in hot water. Add to batter along with salt. Stir in flour, chocolate chips, and nuts. Drop by large spoonfuls onto ungreased pans.
  3. Bake for about 10 minutes in the preheated oven, or until edges are nicely browned.
